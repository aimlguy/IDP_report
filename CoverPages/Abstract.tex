Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) face significant energy inefficiency, marked by high operating costs, substantial dependency on diesel generators (DG), and a lack of predictive monitoring and intelligent optimization frameworks. The absence of digital observability and AI-driven insights leads to considerable energy wastage, peak demand penalties, and uncontrolled environmental footprints. This project addresses these energy challenges by developing an AI-driven digital twin tailored for MSME smart energy optimization. 

The methodology leverages a historical excel dataset, subsequently subjected to MATLAB preprocessing and comprehensive Key Performance Indicator (KPI) computation. Regression modeling constructs the mathematical relationships between production output and energy consumption, establishing the foundation for a digital twin simulation. Through iterative scenario analysis, the virtual model determines optimal load schedules without risk to actual physical production lines, effectively mitigating operational uncertainties.

Experimental evaluation comprised 59 valid observations. Findings establish that the quadratic regression model outperformed the linear model, providing highly accurate prediction capability for industrial energy demand.
