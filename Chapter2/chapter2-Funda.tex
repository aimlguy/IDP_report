\section{Internet of Things Based Energy Monitoring}
The Internet of Things refers to the connection of physical devices, sensors and machines through the internet for data collection and communication. In industrial energy monitoring, IoT devices can measure voltage, current, power, energy consumption and machine operating status.

In an MSME, IoT-based energy monitoring can be used to collect real-time energy data from machines, panels and meters. This data can be sent to a cloud platform or local server for storage and analysis. IoT helps industries move from manual readings to automated data collection.

The main advantages of IoT-based energy monitoring are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Real-time measurement of energy parameters.
    \item Faster identification of abnormal energy consumption.
    \item Reduced manual work and human error.
    \item Better visibility of machine-wise and process-wise energy usage.
    \item Support for AI-based prediction and optimization.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Digital Twin Technology}
A digital twin is a virtual model of a real physical system. It uses data from the real system to represent, analyze and predict the behavior of the physical system. In this project, the physical system is the MSME energy system, and the digital twin is a mathematical and computational model that predicts energy demand and simulates energy-saving scenarios.

A digital twin has three main parts:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Physical System: The actual industry or manufacturing process.
    \item Data Connection: Energy and production data collected through sensors or records.
    \item Virtual Model: A model that analyzes the data and predicts system behavior.
\end{enumerate}

In this project, the digital twin uses production output, grid energy and diesel-generator energy to estimate total energy consumption, cost and emissions. It allows users to test operational decisions virtually before applying them in the real plant.

\section{Machine Learning for Energy Prediction}
Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence that allows systems to learn patterns from data and make predictions. Energy consumption in industries is affected by production quantity, operating time, machine load, day of operation and generator usage. Machine learning can identify these patterns and predict future demand.

The machine learning methods used in this project are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Regression Modeling: Linear and quadratic regression are used to model the relationship between production and energy consumption.
    \item Random Forest: Random Forest is an ensemble learning method that uses multiple decision trees. It is used for energy demand prediction and diesel-generator activation classification.
    \item XGBoost: XGBoost is a gradient boosting technique. It is used to support generator activation prediction using previous energy values.
    \item LSTM: Long Short-Term Memory is a deep learning model used for time-series forecasting. It predicts next-day energy demand using previous energy consumption data.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Energy Performance Indicators}
Energy Performance Indicators are used to measure and compare the energy performance of the MSME. The main KPIs used in this project are:

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Key Performance Indicators}
\label{tab:kpi_list}
\begin{tabular}{|l|p{8cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{KPI} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\hline
Total Energy & Sum of grid energy and DG energy \\
Energy Efficiency & Production output per unit energy consumed \\
Energy Per Unit & Energy consumed per unit of production \\
Operating Cost & Cost of grid energy and DG energy \\
CO2 Emissions & Emissions due to diesel-generator usage \\
DG Share & Percentage of total energy supplied by diesel generator \\
Grid Utilization & Amount of demand handled by grid supply \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

These KPIs help in understanding how efficiently the MSME uses energy and where savings are possible.
