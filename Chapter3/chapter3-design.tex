\section{Dataset and Preprocessing}
The dataset used in this project consists of daily MSME operational data. The main input parameters are production output, grid energy consumption and diesel-generator energy consumption. The data is stored in Microsoft Excel and imported into MATLAB for analysis.

The preprocessing steps are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Import the Excel dataset into MATLAB.
    \item Remove missing values.
    \item Remove negative energy values and invalid production values.
    \item Convert numeric values into a consistent format.
    \item Calculate total energy as the sum of grid energy and DG energy.
    \item Create additional features such as day of week and weekend indicator.
    \item Create a DG activation label where DG Active = 1 if DG energy is greater than zero.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Mathematical Modeling of Energy Consumption}
The first stage of digital twin development is mathematical modeling. The relationship between production and total energy consumption is studied using regression models.

The total energy is calculated as:
\begin{equation}
E_{total} = E_{grid} + E_{DG}
\end{equation}

Energy efficiency is calculated as:
\begin{equation}
Efficiency = \frac{Production}{Total Energy}
\end{equation}

Energy Per Unit is calculated as:
\begin{equation}
EPU = \frac{Total Energy}{Production}
\end{equation}

Operating cost is calculated as:
\begin{equation}
Cost = (E_{grid} \times Tariff_{grid}) + (E_{DG} \times Tariff_{DG})
\end{equation}

Carbon dioxide emissions are calculated as:
\begin{equation}
CO_2 = (\alpha \times E_{DG}) + (\gamma \times E_{Grid})
\end{equation}

The linear regression model is:
\begin{equation}
\hat{E} = mP + c
\end{equation}

The quadratic regression model is:
\begin{equation}
\hat{E} = aP^2 + bP + c
\end{equation}

where $\hat{E}$ is predicted energy, $P$ is production, and $a, b, c$ and $m$ are model constants.

The models are compared using $R^2$ and RMSE. A higher $R^2$ value and lower RMSE value indicate a better prediction model.

\section{Machine Learning Model Design}
Machine learning models are added to improve prediction and decision support.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Random Forest Regressor: The Random Forest Regressor predicts total energy demand using production and calendar-based features. It is useful because it can handle nonlinear data and outliers.
    \item Random Forest Classifier: The Random Forest Classifier predicts whether the diesel generator will be active or not. This helps the factory prepare for possible generator usage.
    \item XGBoost Classifier: XGBoost predicts generator activation using previous energy values. It acts as a second classifier to improve the confidence of DG alert decisions.
    \item LSTM Forecasting Model: LSTM predicts next-day energy demand using recent energy consumption patterns. This supports the AI schedule advisor and load-balancing system.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scenario Simulation Engine}
The scenario simulation engine is the main part of the digital twin. It allows the user to test different operating conditions without affecting the real factory.

The main scenarios are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Energy Prediction Scenario: Predicts energy demand for a given production value.
    \item DG Reduction Scenario: Studies cost and CO2 savings when DG usage is reduced.
    \item Production Increase Scenario: Studies the effect of increased production on energy and cost.
    \item Base-Load Reduction Scenario: Studies energy savings by reducing fixed energy consumption.
    \item Peak Load Analysis: Compares energy performance during peak and non-peak production periods.
\end{enumerate}
This allows MSME managers to understand the effect of operational changes before applying them in the real system.

\section{Dashboard and Decision Support Design}
The dashboard is designed to present the digital twin output in a simple and understandable manner. It includes KPI cards, energy trend graphs, prediction results, anomaly alerts, and what-if simulation tools.

The dashboard contains:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item KPI summary cards for total energy, cost, CO2 and DG share.
    \item Energy trend chart showing grid, DG and total energy consumption.
    \item AI forecast card showing next-day energy prediction.
    \item Load-balancing result showing optimized and unoptimized cost.
    \item Schedule advisor showing suggested machine operating load by time block.
    \item What-if simulator for changing production and viewing live results.
    \item Anomaly detection chart showing abnormal energy days.
\end{enumerate}
